Getting started
1
Pick a template — open the Template section in the left sidebar and choose a known scale to start from.
2
Make it your own — add and delete notes using the sidebar at the left. Double-click notes to edit their labels (note name + octave number, e.g., D3, F#3, Bb3). Drag and drop notes to rearrange them.
3
Export — save your layout as a JSON file, which you can also share with others or use as a backup to import again later. Open Advanced → Pan body to download the current view as image.
Tips
Enharmonics — choose sharp (#) or flat (b) spelling under Advanced.
Undo / Redo — Ctrl+Z / Ctrl+Y, or the buttons in Pan Editing. Other shortcuts helpful shortcuts are als available.
Pan size — drag the radius slider in Advanced to make the canvas larger or smaller.
Chords
1
Click a chord tile — the notes highlight on your pan and play through your speakers.
2
Filter by chord type — use the pills above the list (triads, 7ths, sus, …).
3
Highlight mode — use the Highlight dropdown in the left sidebar to colour notes by note name or chord. Choose 'By Selection' to interactively choose notes and let the Explorer tell you the chord.
Progressions
1
Filter by mood — pick Melancholic, Uplifting, Meditative, … to narrow the list.
2
Click individual chords in a card to highlight and hear them one at a time.
3
Play a sequence — click on the card itself to hear the whole progression.
4
Custom progression — click ＋ Custom, then drag and drop chord tiles to queue them up and create your own progression..
The Root filter narrows results to progressions that start on a note your pan has.
Use Export in the Chords panel to download chord diagrams as a PDF or text sheet.
Click the expand button on the upper right corner of progressions to view them in a larger format in a separate popup.
Getting started
1
Click NEW to create a new tablature.
2
Hover over cells and use shortcuts to set them. Press ? (upper right corner)to show all shortcuts. By learning them, you will be able to create your own tablatures in no time!
3
Press ▶ Play to listen to your tablature.
4
Try yourself out! You can move bars, create sections, copy pieces of tablature, select columns, create lists of favourite tablatures and much more.
